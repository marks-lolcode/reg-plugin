Registration Check-In

Volunteer training guide — badge issuance
Toolbar face: Reggie
No blue "MERCH MODE" banner.
Wrong mode? Tell your lead.

📋 Overview

The extension reads the Neon page, checks whether the attendee can receive their badge, and handles all the typing when you confirm. Your job is to read what the screen shows and follow it. You never need to type badge numbers, dates, or times.

Before you start each shift
  • Confirm the toolbar face is Reggie (not Connie).
  • No blue MERCH MODE banner on any screen.
  • If either is wrong, the extension is in the wrong mode — ask your lead to fix it on the Options page.

🎨 What the icon color means

The Reggie icon in the toolbar changes color to signal status at a glance.

Blue
Good — clear to issue the badge
Yellow
Caution — read the screen before doing anything
Red
Stop — send the attendee to the Help Desk
Grey
Idle — not on a check-in page yet

🪜 Steps

1
Find the attendee in Neon
Search for the attendee by name or account number. Click their name to open their account page. The check-in window appears on its own. If it doesn't appear, click the Reggie icon in the toolbar.
[SCREENSHOT: Neon search results → clicking an account name]
2
Read the account screen
One of these will appear:
  • Green "✓ … Ready for check-in" — the tool moves you forward automatically. Wait a moment.
  • Yellow note(s) — read every note carefully. Tick "I have read and understood the note(s) above", then click Proceed to Check-In →.
  • Red "⛔ SEND TO HELP DESK" — stop. Send the attendee to the Help Desk without issuing anything.
[SCREENSHOT: account screen — yellow note with checkbox]
3
Pick the attendee from the list
The screen shows "Select the attendee to check in:" with a row per person on the registration.
  • Row has a Check In → button → click it.
  • Row says "Send to Help Desk" → that person is blocked. Send them to the Help Desk.
📋 Registration Note
If a note screen appears before the list, read it and click Note Read ✓ to continue.
[SCREENSHOT: attendee list — one green row, one red blocked row]
4
Handle what the check-in screen asks
The screen may show tasks to complete before the badge number appears. Work through them top to bottom.
  • Age verification required (Adult & Dealer badges) — see the detailed walkthrough below ↓
  • Missing emergency contact (ICE) — click Show me the field ↓, fill it in on the form, click Re-check ↺. Details below ↓
  • "NOT ALLOWED — SEND TO HELP DESK" → stop. Send to the Help Desk.
5
Issue the badge
When all issues are clear, the BADGE NUMBER and ticket type appear, and a button activates. See Issuing the Badge ↓.

🪪 Age Verification (Adult & Dealer badges)

Adult and Dealer ticket types require age verification. When this step is active, the screen shows the attendee's legal name and a cutoff date — and blocks the badge until you confirm the ID.

Legal Name Sample, Angela
ID Required DOB on or before 7/3/2007
Age Verified, ID Returned ✓

The cutoff date is calculated automatically each time. You don't need to do the math.

How to verify — step by step
1
Ask for a government-issued photo ID. Driver's license, passport, or state/government ID card. Student IDs and library cards are not sufficient.
2
Confirm the face on the ID matches the person in front of you. If they look significantly different or you have any doubt, tell your lead.
3
Read the date of birth on the ID. Compare it to the "DOB on or before" date shown on screen. If the ID's DOB is on or before that date, they meet the age requirement.
4
Return the ID immediately. Hand it back before you click anything. Do not set it on the desk where it could be forgotten.
5
Click Age Verified, ID Returned ✓ Only click after returning the ID. The screen re-renders and continues.
⛔ Don't click — call your lead if:
  • The ID date of birth is after the cutoff date (too young).
  • The face on the ID doesn't clearly match.
  • The attendee doesn't have a government-issued photo ID.
  • You're unsure about anything on the ID.
[SCREENSHOT: age verification screen showing legal name + DOB cutoff]

🆘 Missing Emergency Contact (ICE)

If the attendee's emergency contact field is blank, a yellow warning appears. You can fix this right at the station without sending them elsewhere.

⚠ How to fix a missing emergency contact
  1. Ask the attendee for their emergency contact's name and phone number.
  2. Click Show me the field ↓ — the field on the Neon form highlights in yellow.
  3. Type the emergency contact info into the highlighted field on the page behind the check-in window.
  4. Click Re-check ↺ in the check-in window. If it saved correctly, the warning clears.

  • Downloads a badge-printer CSV file to the workstation's Downloads folder.
  • Records the pickup date, time, and name in Neon automatically.
  • Shows "Check-In Complete ✓" and resets for the next person.

🚨 When to Call a Manager / Help Desk

Stop and get help if you see any of these:

  • Anything red, or any "Send to Help Desk" / "NOT ALLOWED" message.
  • A hold — Registration Hold, Art Show Hold, or Operations Hold.
  • The ID date of birth is after the cutoff date (attendee is under 18).
  • The face on the ID doesn't clearly match the person.
  • The attendee doesn't have a valid government-issued photo ID.
  • Badge already issued — "ALREADY ISSUED" message on screen.
  • Name mismatch — badge is non-transferable and name doesn't match.
  • Screen says "The badge file didn't download" or "Contact Registration Head."
When in doubt — don't issue the badge. Ask first.
